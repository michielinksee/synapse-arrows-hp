FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about AEO and our services.

What is AEO (AI Engine Optimization)?
AEO stands for AI Engine Optimization. It is the practice of optimizing your brand and products to be accurately cited and recommended by AI search engines such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini. While traditional SEO targets Google's web crawlers, AEO addresses AI models' knowledge graphs and citation logic. This includes implementing structured data (JSON-LD), clarifying entities, optimizing knowledge panels, and designing AI-readable content.
How is the KanseiLINK AEO Score calculated?
The KanseiLINK AEO Score is calculated by combining MCP compatibility, API quality, documentation completeness, authentication guide clarity, and actual AI agent success rate data. Over 11,000 services are quantitatively evaluated and rated on a 9-level scale from AAA (highest) to CCC (lowest). Scores are updated quarterly to reflect the latest compatibility status.
What does your cross-border consulting cover?
Based in Singapore, we support Japanese companies entering Southeast Asian markets and Southeast Asian companies entering the Japanese market. We provide end-to-end support from market research, local partner development, and event support to B2B lead generation. We are especially strong in Japanese food and beverage overseas expansion, offering practical support backed by SakeNavi's market analysis data.
What is SakeNavi (ScaNavi)?
SakeNavi is an app where you simply scan a Japanese sake label with your smartphone and an AI sommelier explains the brand details, flavor profile, and food pairings. Available on iOS, Android, and Web. It also publishes English articles on export trends, tariffs, and market analysis for overseas sake buyers and restaurants, functioning as a B2B lead generation tool.
What is Linksee Memory?
Linksee Memory is a local-first memory MCP server for AI agents. It runs entirely locally on SQLite with no account registration or API keys required. Multiple LLMs including Claude Code, Cursor, ChatGPT, and Gemini can share a single memory file, eliminating context loss between sessions. It is published as open-source software under the MIT License.

For specific technical challenges, we can provide spot consulting — feel free to reach out.
